inContext Online profiles built for you.

Social media sites give you a template of who they think you are, ask you to fill it in, and then sell your information off to advertisers. inContext lets you define your profile on your own terms, and gives you control over who sees your information. With the power of contexts, the way people see you is up to you.

Make your page

How does it work?

Your page is made of lenses. You name each lens, and decide what aspect of your identity it should reflect. Whether it's your education, music interests, or photos of you with your family, each lens is a different view of your identity.

When you make a lens, it's just an empty page. To flesh it out, add some cards, the most basic unit of the page. You give them a title and they can hold an image, some text, or both. Cards let you describe yourself however you'd like.

Once you've made your lenses and added some cards, it's time to define your contexts. Contexts are how people get to know you. Instead of asking to be your friend, people ask to connect with you in one of your contexts. Friend, co-worker, band-mate, potential client -- your contexts are up to you. A context defines which of your lenses somebody who knows you in that context can see. If you want one of your lenses to only show up for close friends, or you want to have a simple context just for acquaintances, you can do it.
