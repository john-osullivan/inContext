inContext Online profiles built for you.

Social media sites give you a template of who they think you are, ask you to fill it in, and then sell your information off to advertisers. inContext lets you define your profile on your own terms, and gives you control over who sees your information. With the power of contexts, the way people see you is up to you.

Make your page

How does it work?

In one sentence: You define aspects of yourself, add details about them, and make contexts to share them with.

Scroll down for more detail!

Aspects

Your page is made of aspects. Each aspect is one screen of your profile, and can describe any part of your identity you like. Whether it's:

  • Favorite foods!
  • Courses you're taking
  • Personal Goals
  • Silly pictures of you with sock puppets
An aspect can be any part of you that you want.

Details

When you make an aspect, it's just an empty page. To flesh it out, add some details! You name them and can add an image, some text, or both. Details let you describe yourself however you'd like.

Want to share pictures of your friends? Go for it.

Need everyone to know what League you reached in Starcraft last season? Sounds good!

Whichever of the details of your life you want to record, you can do it.

Contexts

Once you've made your aspects and added some details, it's time to define your contexts. Contexts are how people get to know you.

Instead of asking to be your friend, people ask to connect with you in one of your contexts. Friend, co-worker, band-mate, potential client -- your contexts are up to you.

A context is a set of aspects that you want visible for somebody who knows you like that. If you want one of your aspects to only show up for close friends, or you want to have a simple context just for acquaintances, you can make them that way!.
